## 1️⃣ Book Basics

* **Title**: 1984  
* **Author**: George Orwell  
* **Purpose of Reading**: Personal growth / Understanding reality  
* **Reading Period**: (to be filled)

### One-Line Summary (My Version)

> This book asked me:  
> **“In a world where even thoughts are controlled, can I protect myself until the end?”**  
> and at the same time,  
> **“How many things have I already accepted without questioning?”**

---

## 2️⃣ Why This Book

* What made me choose this book:  
  I had heard many times that dystopian works are not just imagination,  
  but closely resemble reality, so I felt this book would have a deeper meaning.

* How does it connect to who I am / my mission now?:  
  Since it deals with human thought and freedom,  
  I felt it was not just reading, but **a process of examining how I see myself and society.**

* What problem or emotion did I expect before reading?:  
  I wanted to understand how humans change in a controlled society,  
  and eventually how they break down.

---

## 3️⃣ Problem I Resonated With

> ❗ **Important**  
> The “problem” here is not the author’s problem,  
> but **my own problem that I rediscovered through this book**.

* A situation / sentence / concept that stayed with me:  
  People being constantly watched,  
  yet accepting it naturally without questioning

* How did this connect to my own experience?:  
  I also had moments where I accepted social norms or expectations  
  without thinking deeply, just assuming “that’s how things are.”

* Why does this feel stronger for a timid person?:  
  Because questioning or challenging something feels uncomfortable,  
  making it easier to conform and give up the chance to think independently

→ This book did not just criticize society,  
but revealed **how easily I stop thinking myself.**

---

## 4️⃣ Emotional Response

* My state before reading:  
  Light curiosity and interest in dystopian stories

* Strongest emotions while reading:  
  Growing anxiety,  
  frustration from a system with no escape,  
  and a chilling sense that “this could actually be possible”

* What emotion remained after finishing the book?:  
  Not just shock,  
  but **an uncomfortable awareness that made me question reality more deeply**

→ It was an unsettling feeling, but also one that felt necessary.

---

## 5️⃣ Key Insight

* One important truth this book taught me:  
  **If you control information, you can ultimately control human thought and perception**

* A perspective I hadn’t seen before:  
  Surveillance does not just restrict behavior,  
  but **destroys the foundation of thinking itself**

* A sentence I needed the most right now:  
  The possibility that “truth itself can disappear”

**Quote (Optional)**  
> “Freedom is the freedom to say that two plus two make four.”

→ This felt less like a statement about math,  
and more like **the right to perceive reality as it truly is.**

---

## 6️⃣ Mission Connection

* How does this connect to my mission of **“giving courage to timid people”**?:  
  This work shows that even before action,  
  **thinking and questioning itself is already an act of courage.**

* Similarities / differences between the book’s idea of courage and mine:  
  Similarity: both begin with small internal resistance  
  Difference: this book shows that even that courage can collapse

* If I recommend this to a timid person, why?:  
  Because it helps them realize how dangerous it is  
  to live without questioning anything.

## 7️⃣ From Reading to Creating

Things I could create inspired by this book:

* Game idea:  
  A psychological game where you must protect your own thoughts while avoiding surveillance

* Character:  
  Someone who outwardly conforms completely,  
  but constantly questions internally

* Service:  
  A platform that presents multiple perspectives  
  and encourages users to think for themselves

* System:  
  A structure where memory, records, and truth change based on choices

* Message structure:  
  “If you don’t think, you will be thought for.”

>   became the core gameplay mechanic?

  When Winston begins thinking on his own,  
  but struggles to turn those thoughts into action

* What part of me did this book touch?:

  yet still choose the safer option

* A small change in my thinking or attitude:

  that I used to accept as “normal”
